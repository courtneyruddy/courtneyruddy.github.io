The Rising Rate of Sexual Misconduct at the
University of Maryland, College Park

Photo from Britannica

Since 2014, the number of sexual assaults reported to the University of Maryland, College Park has more than doubled. For the 2021-2022 cycling period, 283 sexual assault misconduct cases were reported to campus administration, and 23 led to investigations.

Although administration has not outright said that there is a problem with sexual assault, in fact they have even denied such allegations, the statistics paint a different story.

The number of reports made have been on a slow increase since 2014. Although the pandemic was able to decrease the amount of reports, the moment things returned to normal as did the misconduct statistics.

One former student endured some of her own experiences with rape culture and misconduct within the Greek Life community affiliated with the University. Lucy Taylor started a podcast after she disaffiliated from her sorority. She gets completely honest with her audiences about what she has witnessed, and speaks to others with similar experiences.

Lucy discusses the origin of her podcast, “I released season one of Snapped, which was like a tell-all about my experience.” She continues, “It was an outlet for me”

It became almost like a research project for her. It wasn’t only an outlet for her, it was for her listeners as well. “There's no podcast out there interviewing the survivors of the greek life system” She continues, “People are so relieved to tell these stories and feel like they’re not delusional.”

Lucy also heeds a warning to young students to avoid the fraternity parties, although acknowledging how large a part of the social scene they play. She gives advice then to the school on ways that the could create a space in which Greek Life wasn’t so heavily relied upon for social interaction.

Lucy states, “The advice to the school that I would give to UMD would be to create more appealing communal spaces. Make some places that students can hang out or lounges or something. There’s not that many aesthetically pleasing spaces to hang out in.”

Many Fraternity parties occur in houses that are not run by the University of Maryland, making them considered off-campus assaults. Almost half of the reported assaults for last year’s period were off-campus assaults.

The types of reports under the sexual misconduct category varied, however the most commonly reported types of reports were sexual harassment and sexual assault-non-consensual sexual penetration.

While the increase in the amount of reports over the years has more than doubled, the number of investigations have not. Of course there are situations in which other measures are taken, however over the years the percentage of reports that are investigated has decreased.

According to the Rape, Abuse, Incest National Network, otherwise know as RAINN, their statistics show that only 31% of sexual assaults are reported. For women college-aged, 32% of non-students report, and only 20% of students do. Looking at UMD’s statistics from that perspective, that would mean almost 1,000 sexual misconduct cases reported and unreported last year with 23 investigations.

Lucy made it clear to call out the University of Maryland for not doing their jobs in protecting women, creating safe spaces for women to tell their stories, and locations on campus for students to have fun without the threat of assault.
